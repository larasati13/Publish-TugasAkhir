ABOUT RISOL

Risoles is a popular Indonesian snack originating from the 13th century. It has a delicate, savory flavor which makes risoles ideal for breakfast or an afternoon snack. Risoles consists of a small croquette that is typically wrapped in pastry, rolled in breadcrumbs, then deep-fried until golden brown in color. Usually, it is filled with ingredients such as seafood, minced meat, or vegetables. Sometimes, it can be filled with sweet ingredients and served as a dessert. The name of the dish stems from the traditional Portuguese rissole. This tasty snack can be found throughout Indonesia, in numerous markets, bakeries, and on street stands.
